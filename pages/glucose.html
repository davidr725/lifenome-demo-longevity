What is Glucose?

Glucose is a simple sugar that serves as the primary source of energy for your body's cells. When you eat, your digestive system breaks down carbohydrates into glucose, which enters your bloodstream. Your body carefully regulates blood glucose levels through hormones like insulin and glucagon.

Blood glucose testing measures the amount of sugar in your blood at a specific moment. This test is essential for diagnosing and monitoring diabetes, prediabetes, and other metabolic conditions. Consistently elevated glucose levels can indicate that your body isn't processing sugar effectively.

Healthcare providers typically order glucose tests as part of routine screenings, to diagnose diabetes, or to monitor blood sugar control in people with known diabetes or prediabetes.
