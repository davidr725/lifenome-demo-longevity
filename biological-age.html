Understanding Blood-Based Biological Age

Biological age based on blood biomarker data refers to an estimate of an individual's age derived from physiological and molecular measurements obtained from blood samples. This approach evaluates various biomarkers that can indicate the body's condition, including but not limited to hormones, inflammatory markers, blood sugar levels, lipid profiles, and markers of organ function.

These biomarkers reflect different aspects of health, physiological resilience, and the aging process, providing insights into the biological state of the body compared to the chronological age. Biological age based on blood biomarkers is significant because it can provide actionable insights into an individual's health status beyond what chronological age can tell.
